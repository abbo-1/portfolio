My Background

How I Got Here

My passion for technology started as something inherited watching my older brother build and fix computers. School hours were spent doodling in the margins, With his guidance, I built my first PC in 7th grade.

Primary motivation: the ability to play games like Starcraft, Max Payne, Unreal Tournament, and Fallout 2.

2001

Me in my room with my first computer.

Note: the super cool, and not at all nerdy, signed George Takei photo in the background.




PC gaming opened the door to technology, but once I went through it there was no turning back. In high school I tested for my A+ certification and enrolled in networking classes as electives. I also got my first taste of web development, learning basic HTML and CSS.




One of my very first jobs out of high school was at a local community college. I managed a computer lab- honing my own ability to diagnose, fix, and maintain three dozen machines. It's a job I still look back on fondly.

As I got older I moved onto other interests- eventually deciding to finish my Bachelor's degree where I graduated with a 4.0. Going into the job market I saw the need for programmers and decided (for lack of a better phrase) it was time to come home.

I invested my spare time in learning to code through online tutorials. Eventually I discovered digitalcrafts, where I spent the next few months deeply immersed in a variety of programming languages.
